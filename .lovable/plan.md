# JEEnie — Final Co-Founder Plan

## First, the 35-min question — you're right, I was wrong

35 min/day is not JEE prep. That's Duolingo. Real numbers from the space:

| Student type | Daily study | JEEnie share (realistic) |
|---|---|---|
| Class 11 (starting) | 4–5 hrs | 60–90 min |
| Class 12 (serious) | 6–8 hrs | 90–150 min |
| Dropper | 10–12 hrs | 2–4 hrs |
| NEET aspirant | 6–10 hrs | 90–180 min |

**Mission blocks must be 45–90 min each, not 5–20.** A JEE student cannot fix a chapter in 20 minutes. New mission shape:

```
TODAY'S MISSION            2 hr 30 min
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
BLOCK 1  Learn + Practice        75 min
  Rotation — theory recap + 25 Qs
BLOCK 2  Revision                45 min
  Atomic Structure — 15 Qs + PYQs
BLOCK 3  Weak-spot fix           30 min
  Kinematics: 10 targeted Qs
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[ START BLOCK 1 ]   Break after each
```
Companion mode = 60–120 min. Guided mode = 90–180 min. Dropper mode = up to 4 hrs, split into 3 sessions with forced breaks. **Student sets daily hours once; JEEnie fills them intelligently.**

## Co-founder's honest take on the EdTech landscape

I researched what killed / stalled the players you're up against. Pattern:

- **Physics Wallah** — cheap + community. Product is average, price is the moat. You can't beat them on price.
- **Unacademy / Vedantu** — recorded lectures + live classes. Bloated, no personalization. Losing users fast.
- **Embibe** — had the best "AI adaptive" story on paper. Died on execution: too complex UI, unclear value, enterprise pivot.
- **Allen Digital / Aakash iTutor** — brand + offline, digital is an afterthought.
- **Doubtnut** — nailed one wedge (photo → solution). Got acquired. Wedge > platform.
- **Toppr** — tried to be everything. Sold to Byju's, died.

**What every one of them lacks:** a product that *decides for the student*. They all give menus. You give a mission.

**What none of them own:** the "companion to coaching" position. 90% of serious aspirants attend Allen/Aakash/PW/local coaching. None of those apps help you practice *what your coaching taught today*. This is the wedge.

## The positioning (this is the whole strategy)

> **"JEEnie is not another coaching app. It's your personal AI coach that works alongside your coaching."**

Not "learn from JEEnie." → "**Prep smarter with JEEnie.**"

This does three things:
1. **No enemy** — coaching institutes don't fight you, they might even recommend you.
2. **Zero switching cost** — students don't have to leave anything. They add JEEnie.
3. **Clear job** — "the app that tells me what to practice after class."

Guided mode still exists for confused/self-study students, but Companion mode is your **go-to-market wedge**.

## The final product — 4 sharp pillars

### Pillar 1: Mission Engine (the brain)
- One decision every day. Never a menu.
- 60–180 min blocks based on user's set daily hours.
- Mode-aware: Guided / Companion / Dropper / NEET.
- Every task has a **"Why this?"** one-liner → trust.

### Pillar 2: Companion Layer (the wedge)
- "Log today's class" — 3-tap: subject → chapter → done.
- JEEnie instantly builds practice + revision loop around it.
- Voice log ("aaj rotation padha") — you already have Whisper.
- **Killer feature: "Class Recap Test"** — 10 Qs on today's class, right after logging. 15 min. Student sees exactly what stuck and what didn't. Nothing else in the market does this.

### Pillar 3: Adaptive Practice (the moat)
- Real per-topic readiness (0–100), not weak/strong labels.
- Ebbinghaus revision — questions surface before student forgets.
- Difficulty rises as accuracy rises, drops when they struggle. Uses existing question bank tags.
- Every wrong answer → JEEnie Bhai auto-offers explanation (already built).

### Pillar 4: Predictive Coach (the retention)
- Home shows: *"On track → 98.4 percentile. Miss today → 97.8."* Every day.
- Proactive nudges (push): *"3 din se Physics zyada, kal Chem"* / *"Rotation revision due, 30 min de do"*.
- Weekly report (Sunday push): what improved, what slipped, next week's focus.

**Concept maps / chapter summaries** — noted, parked for phase 2.

## Growth & business plan (the co-founder part)

### Wedge → Expand strategy
1. **Wedge**: Companion mode for Allen/Aakash/PW students. One city first (Kota or your city).
2. **Land**: Free tier = 1 mission/day + 20 Qs + 3 doubt solves. Enough to feel the magic.
3. **Expand**: Guided mode for self-study students in tier-2/3 cities.
4. **Own**: When students trust JEEnie's plan more than their coaching's, you're the platform.

### Pricing (research-backed for Indian EdTech)
- **Free** — 1 mission/day, 20 Qs, 3 JEEnie Bhai queries, streak, roast, share
- **Pro ₹199/mo or ₹1,499/yr** — unlimited missions, unlimited Qs, unlimited doubts, prediction engine, class-log unlimited
- **Pro+ ₹399/mo or ₹2,999/yr** — everything + PYQ vault + mock analysis + parent weekly summary + priority

Anchor: Physics Wallah = ₹4,200/yr. Unacademy = ₹40k+. You slot as *"the affordable smart layer everyone uses on top of their existing prep."*

### Distribution (the actual hard part — do these in order)
1. **Roast + Share cards** — already built. Make them insanely shareable, JEE meme culture. This is your organic acquisition. Every roast card = a free ad.
2. **Coaching-teacher partnerships** — free Pro accounts for coaching teachers, they recommend to their batch. You're not competition, you're their homework tool. Start with 20 teachers in one city.
3. **Telegram/Discord communities** — JEE aspirants live there. Get 3 mods to try JEEnie, feature them.
4. **YouTube shorts** — "JEEnie predicted my JEE percentile" type content. Cheap, viral, category-defining.
5. **Reddit r/JEENEETards, r/JEE** — do NOT spam. Show up as a student, share the tool honestly.
6. **Only after 10k active users**: Google Ads, influencer deals. Not before.

### Metrics that matter (ignore the rest)
- **D7 retention ≥ 40%** — non-negotiable. If students don't come back in week 1, product is broken.
- **DAU/MAU ≥ 0.5** — study apps live or die here.
- **Mission completion rate ≥ 60%** — proves the AI is picking right.
- **Free → Pro conversion ≥ 5%** — good for edtech at ₹199 price.
- **NPS from Class 12 droppers** — they're the highest-intent segment.

### What NOT to do (I've seen these kill EdTech startups)
- ❌ Don't build live classes. You lose. Zoom is a commodity.
- ❌ Don't hire teachers. Content is not your moat, decisions are.
- ❌ Don't chase B2B (schools/coaching institutes) in year 1. Long sales, kills focus.
- ❌ Don't add parent app / educator dashboard yet. Focus.
- ❌ Don't localize to 10 languages. Hindi + English is 95% of your market.
- ❌ Don't raise money before D7 retention proves out. You'll burn it on ads that don't convert.
- ❌ Don't touch NEET until JEE flywheel spins. Same product, but split focus = death.

## The 12-week execution plan (point by point, in order)

### Weeks 1–2: Kill the menus, ship the mission
1. Add `profiles.prep_mode` (`guided` / `companion` / `dropper`) + onboarding question
2. Add `profiles.daily_study_minutes` (user picks: 60 / 90 / 120 / 180 / 240)
3. Create `daily_missions` table + GRANTs + RLS
4. Build `generate-daily-mission` edge function (deterministic v1, no LLM):
   - Reads mode + daily minutes + readiness + spaced-repetition due list
   - Composes 2–4 blocks of 45–90 min each
5. New `MissionHome.tsx` replaces current `/` landing
6. Hide manual planner from primary nav (keep route, remove entry)
7. "Why this?" one-liner on every block (rule-based, not LLM)

### Weeks 3–4: Companion wedge
8. `class_logs` table + GRANTs + RLS
9. 3-tap "Log today's class" chip on Home (Companion mode)
10. Voice log via existing Whisper function
11. **Class Recap Test** — 10-Q auto-quiz on logged chapter, right there
12. Mission generator reads `class_logs` and prioritizes today's class + spaced revision

### Weeks 5–6: The brain
13. `src/services/readinessEngine.ts` — 0–100 per topic + label (Stable/Improving/Needs Attention/Critical)
14. `src/services/revisionEngine.ts` — Ebbinghaus decay + `predicted_forget_date` column
15. `src/services/capacityEngine.ts` — learns focus window, best time of day
16. Mission generator switches from deterministic → readiness-driven
17. Adaptive difficulty: pick from existing difficulty tags based on running accuracy

### Weeks 7–8: Predictive coach
18. `src/services/predictionEngine.ts` — expected percentile / NEET score
19. Home shows live prediction + "if you complete today" delta
20. `coach-nudges` edge function → banner + push after every session
21. Sunday weekly-report push
22. Empty state everywhere → "next best action" from mission engine

### Weeks 9–10: Retention & polish
23. Roast/Share cards redesigned with proper JEEnie brand (fix reported issues)
24. Streak recovery, streak freeze — cheap, huge retention wins
25. Leaderboards scoped to class/city (not global — global demotivates)
26. Push-notification schedule: morning mission, evening reminder, streak save
27. Onboarding cut to 3 screens max

### Weeks 11–12: Growth loop
28. Referral code inside every roast card ("your friend beat you — try JEEnie")
29. WhatsApp share with pre-filled message + auto-generated card
30. First 20 coaching-teacher partnerships (manual, you do it yourself)
31. First YouTube short + first Telegram community drop
32. Instrument analytics for D7, DAU/MAU, mission completion — dashboards for you, not users
